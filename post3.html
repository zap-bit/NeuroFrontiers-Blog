Intelligence Isn't Trained. It's Formed Under Pressure.

April 2026 · 6 min read

In 2025, researchers at Google DeepMind and HHMI Janelia Research Campus published a whole-body physics simulation of Drosophila melanogaster in Nature. The body of an animal influences how its nervous system generates behaviour. Accurately modelling neural control of sensorimotor behaviour requires an anatomically detailed biomechanical representation of the body. Using data-driven reinforcement learning trained on real fly movement data, the virtual fly can walk and fly just like the real thing, walking and flying across complex trajectories, using its eyes to control and steer its flight.

The framing matters here. This was not a system explicitly programmed with locomotion rules. The behavior emerged from the interaction of a neural controller, a physical body model, and environmental constraints. The body of an animal determines how the nervous system produces behavior.

Behavior lives in the whole stack, not in the nervous system alone. Influence runs in every direction. FlyWire Consortium, Nature 2024 · Vaxenburg et al., Nature 2025

The scale is also worth pausing on. The Drosophila brain contains almost 140,000 neurons and over 15 million synaptic connections, confirmed by the FlyWire Consortium's complete connectome, published in Nature in 2024. The central nervous system, brain plus ventral nerve cord, contains about 160,000 neurons in total. The human brain operates at approximately 86 billion. And yet, at this comparatively modest scale, coherent and adaptive behavior emerges.

This is the core claim of embodied cognition: behavior is not inserted into a system. It arises from the system's interaction with its body, its environment, and its noise. The environment matters. The body matters. Constraint matters.

The contrast

This stands in contrast to how most modern AI systems are built. Contemporary architectures are trained on curated datasets, filtered, labeled, and pre-structured before the system encounters them. The model optimizes within a representational lens designed in advance. Biological intelligence, by contrast, forms under environmental pressure, shaped by necessity and feedback with a physical world rather than by prior curation.

The filter step on the right is the crux. The world is intercepted before it reaches the system.

A parallel in physics

Topological qubits store quantum information in the topological properties of a physical system rather than in the properties of individual particles or atoms, and this makes them theoretically more stable than any other qubit engineered to date. In February 2025, Microsoft unveiled Majorana 1, the world's first Quantum Processing Unit powered by a Topological Core, with quantum information encoded in Majorana Zero Modes, protected by topology, and processed through measurements.

The principle is structurally analogous to what we see in biological systems: because the electrons don't exist in any one location, their information is protected topologically from any local disturbances. Robustness is not added on top of function. It is intrinsic to the architecture.

The braided green line threads through noise that cannot reach it. Microsoft Majorana 1, Nature 2025 · claims remain under peer review

It should be noted that Microsoft's claims remain contested. Outside researchers have critiqued the evidence for Majorana-based topological protection as ambiguous. The principle is scientifically grounded; the engineering demonstration is still being verified.

The question this raises

If behavior emerges from structure and constraint, and robustness comes from computing through uncertainty rather than despite it, then the relevant question for AI development is not only how to improve accuracy within existing training paradigms.

It is whether the conditions under which intelligence is asked to form are the right ones at all.
